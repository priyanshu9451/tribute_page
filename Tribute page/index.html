Who was Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Vallabhbhai Jhaverbhai Patel
31 October 1875– 15 December 1950, commonly known as Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel,[a] was an Indian independence nationalist and barrister who served as the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of India from 1947 to 1950. He was a senior leader of the Indian National Congress, who played a significant role in the country's struggle for independence and its political integration. In India and elsewhere, he was often called Sardar, meaning "Chief" in Hindi, Urdu, Bengali and Persian. He acted as the Home Minister during the political integration of India and the Indo-Pakistani War of 1947.

  • After India was free from British rule, Sardar Patel worked hard to bring the entire country together for a strong national integration. This is why he is called the ‘Iron Man of India’.
  • He was the recipient of the Bharat Ratna Award in 1991. As a national leader, he firmly believed in the power of unity. It was this belief that helped him bring divided India together. His birth anniversary is observed as ‘Rashtriya Ekta Divas’ or the ‘National Unity Day in India so that we never forget his message of strength in being united.

Major Life Events

1918

Kheda Satyagraha (1918): -Patel led the Kheda Satyagraha, a non-violent protest against the oppressive policies of the British during the plague epidemic. The farmers were facing heavy taxes.

1928

Bardoli Satyagraha (1928):-In response to the unfair tax levied on farmers in Bardoli, Patel organized a successful satyagraha.The struggle earned him the title "Sardar."

1942

Quit India Movement (1942): -Patel supported the Quit India Movement initiated by Mahatma Gandhi. He, along with other leaders, was imprisoned by the British authorities.

1947

Accession of Junagadh (1947):-Patel played a key role in the accession of Junagadh to India.Despite the Nawab's desire to accede to Pakistan, Patel ensured the region became a part of India.

1947

First Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister (1947):-Patel was appointed as the first Deputy Prime Minister and the Home Minister of independent India.

1947-1949

Integration of Princely States (1947-1949):-After India gained independence, Patel was given the monumental task of integrating over 500 princely states into the newly formed nation.
